Proper nutrition and consistent schedules are key to maintaining health, reducing stress, and providing a high standard of care. This guide covers best practices for meal routines, handling special diets, hygiene protocols, and how software helps track nutrition and parent communication.

Why Feeding Protocols Matter

\n

According to the American Kennel Club and WSAVA Nutrition Guidelines, structured feeding in kennels and daycares helps ensure:

\n
    \n
  • Health: Meeting nutritional needs prevents underfeeding or overfeeding.
    • \n
  • Consistency: Following the same routine reduces digestive upset.
    • \n
  • Stress reduction: Regular mealtimes create predictability for dogs in new environments.
    • \n
  • Transparency: Parents value knowing their dog’s diet is carefully managed.
    • \n
\n\n

Feeding Schedules

\n

Different life stages require different schedules:

\n
    \n
  • Puppies: 3–4 small meals per day to support growth and prevent hypoglycemia.
    • \n
  • Adults: 2 meals per day is typical; portion sizes based on weight and activity.
    • \n
  • Seniors: 2 meals daily, sometimes smaller portions to aid digestion.
    • \n
\n

Always align with pet parent instructions and document exceptions for medical or lifestyle reasons.

\n\n

Handling Special Diets

\n
    \n
  • Allergies: Strict labeling and separation to prevent cross-contamination.
    • \n
  • Prescription diets: Store separately and follow precise vet instructions.
    • \n
  • Raw diets: Use designated prep areas, sanitize thoroughly, and freeze properly.
    • \n
  • Supplements: Document and administer only as directed.
    • \n
\n\n \n\n

Food Storage & Hygiene

\n
    \n
  • Store dry kibble in sealed, food-grade containers.
    • \n
  • Label each bag or container with the dog’s name and feeding instructions.
    • \n
  • Keep raw food frozen until use; thaw in the refrigerator, not at room temperature.
    • \n
  • Wash bowls after every meal to prevent bacterial growth.
    • \n
  • Maintain a dedicated area for food prep, away from cleaning supplies.
    • \n
\n\n

Staff Training for Feeding

\n
    \n
  • Portioning: Use measuring cups or scales to prevent under/overfeeding.
    • \n
  • Supervision: Observe mealtimes to prevent food aggression or sharing.
    • \n
  • Record keeping: Document what was eaten and note refusals.
    • \n
  • Emergency protocols: Train staff to respond to choking or allergic reactions.
    • \n
\n\n

Using Software to Manage Feeding

\n

Dog daycare software, boarding tools, and kennel management platforms streamline feeding management by:

\n
    \n
  • Logging dietary requirements per pet.
    • \n
  • Alerting staff to allergies or special instructions.
    • \n
  • Recording meal completion and refusals.
    • \n
  • Communicating updates to pet parents.
    • \n
  • Tracking inventory and reordering supplies.
    • \n
  • Extending to catteries for feline diets.
    • \n

FAQs

\n

How often should dogs be fed in daycare or boarding?

\n

Puppies usually need 3–4 meals daily, adults typically 2, and seniors 2 smaller meals. Always follow parent instructions.

\n\n

How should facilities handle allergies?

\n

Use strict labeling, separate storage, and dedicated utensils. Document allergy details in software to prevent mistakes.

\n\n

Is raw feeding safe in boarding facilities?

\n

Yes, if strict hygiene protocols are followed. Use separate prep areas, sanitize surfaces, and store raw diets in sealed containers.

\n\n

What about food aggression in group settings?

\n

Feed dogs individually or in small groups under supervision to prevent conflicts. Never leave food unattended.

\n\n

How does software help with feeding management?

\n

Software tracks diets, logs meal completion, flags allergies, and communicates updates directly to pet parents, improving accuracy and trust.

\n\n

Conclusion & Next Steps

\n

Feeding and nutrition are central to dog health and happiness in daycare and boarding. By creating clear routines, training staff, and leveraging kennel management software, daycare tools, and boarding software, facilities can deliver safe, consistent care and peace of mind for pet parents. Start by reviewing your feeding protocols, training your team, and digitizing records to ensure every dog gets the nutrition they need.
