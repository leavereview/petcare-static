Why Daycare Needs Different Features Than Boarding

\n

Boarding prioritizes runs/suites and overnight stays; daycare is fast-paced and repetitive. The biggest operational differences:

\n
    \n
  • Recurring attendance: Weekly routines demand packages/credits and bulk scheduling.
    • \n
  • Ratios over rooms: Safety depends on staff-to-dog ratios and compatible playgroups.
    • \n
  • Speed at check-in: Mornings are intense; scanning, tap-to-check-in, and portal prework are critical.
    • \n
  • Communication volume: More frequent reminders, renewals, and behavior notes.
    • \n
\n

Software built with daycare in mind reduces friction, protects safety, and keeps utilization high.

\n\n

Feature Deep-Dive (What to Look For)

\n\n

1) Packages, Passes & Auto-Decrement

\n

Daycare success depends on recurring visits. Your system should sell and track 5-, 10-, or monthly passes; decrement automatically at check-in; and prompt renewal before credits run out.

\n\n

2) Playgroup, Capacity & Ratios

\n

Define groups (e.g., Puppies, Small, Large, Seniors), set maximums, and enforce staff-to-dog ratios. Bonus points for temperament tags and “only with sibling” pairing.

\n\n

3) Vaccination Tracking & Reminders

\n

Owners upload certificates; the system warns about expiries well before the next visit — preventing awkward denials at the door.

\n\n

4) Owner Portal & Mobile Experience

\n

Self-service profile updates, waiver e-sign, package balances, and payment history. Everything should work beautifully on a phone.

\n\n

5) Fast Check-In & Attendance

\n

Search, scan, tap-to-check-in, and bulk actions for the morning rush. Notes and behavior flags should be visible at a glance.

\n\n

6) Reminders & Renewals

\n

Automated SMS/email for upcoming bookings, package balances, and renewal prompts. Smart templates save staff time.

\n\n

7) Upsells & Add-Ons

\n

Offer nail trims, enrichment sessions, or training at checkout or check-in. These small add-ons compound into meaningful revenue.

\n\n

8) Reporting That Matters

\n

Utilization by group/hour, revenue by product, repeat rate, and top customers. Export to CSV for accounting or marketing.
