What Is Cattery Software?

\n

Cattery software is a specialized tool for managing cat boarding facilities. It's similar to kennel or dog boarding software, but includes cat-specific management tools. Key features usually include:

\n
    \n
  • Online booking & payments: Pet parents can reserve a cattery suite and pay online.
    • \n
  • Pet records: Vaccination history, diet notes, medication reminders, behavior preferences.
    • \n
  • Owner portal: Clients update records, upload vaccine certificates, view invoices.
    • \n
  • Feeding & medication schedules: Track special diets or prescriptions.
    • \n
  • Automated reminders: Notify owners about upcoming bookings or vaccinations.
    • \n
  • Reporting: Monitor occupancy, revenue, and trends.
    • \n
\n

Some platforms support multi-service facilities (boarding dogs and cats), while others are streamlined for catteries only.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cattery Software

\n\n

What is cattery software and how is it different from kennel software?

\n

Cattery software is specifically designed for cat boarding facilities with features tailored to feline care, including FIV/FeLV testing tracking, individual housing preferences, feeding schedules for finicky eaters, and stress monitoring. While kennel software focuses on dogs with group play and exercise tracking, cattery software emphasizes quiet environments, litter box monitoring, and cat-specific health protocols that differ from canine care requirements.

\n\n

What cat-specific features should cattery software have?

\n

Essential cat-specific features include individual housing assignment (cats rarely share spaces), FIV/FeLV test result tracking, detailed feeding schedules for special diets, medication reminders for chronic conditions common in cats, litter box monitoring logs, stress level observations, and the ability to track whether cats are indoor-only (affecting outdoor enclosure usage). The software should also manage quiet time requirements and minimal handling preferences for anxious cats.

\n\n

How does cattery software track FIV/FeLV testing records?

\n

Cattery software stores digital copies of FIV/FeLV test results on each cat's profile, tracks test dates and expiration periods, and sends automatic alerts when tests are approaching expiration (typically annually). The system can flag cats without current test results during booking, prevent check-ins for non-compliant cats, and generate reports showing which cats need updated testing before their next stay.

\n\n

Can cattery software manage dietary requirements for cats?

\n

Yes, cattery software manages complex feline dietary needs including prescription diets for urinary, kidney, or digestive issues, feeding schedules (many cats need multiple small meals), food allergies and preferences, wet versus dry food specifications, and portion sizes by weight. Staff receive feeding reminders with specific instructions for each cat, and the system tracks which cats require separated feeding times to prevent food aggression.

\n\n

How much does cattery management software cost?

\n

Cattery software typically costs $40-150 per month depending on features and facility size. Entry-level plans ($40-60/month) cover basic booking and records, mid-tier plans ($70-100/month) add automated reminders and owner portals, while premium plans ($100-150/month) include advanced reporting, multi-location support, and integrated payment processing. Many providers offer free trials to test cat-specific features before committing.
