The 10 Must-Have Features of Dog Boarding Software

\n

1) 24/7 Online Booking

\n

Pet parents should be able to book anytime from desktop or mobile. Look for support for deposits, packages, and recurring stays. Approvals and blackout dates are helpful during peak season.

\n

2) Vaccination Tracking with Automated Reminders

\n

Compliance is non-negotiable. Your system should allow uploading certificates, flag expiring vaccines, and send automatic reminders to owners ahead of check-in.

\n

3) Owner Portal (Self-Service)

\n

A secure portal cuts admin by letting clients update pet details, upload proof of vaccination, sign waivers, and pay invoices — without calling the desk.

\n

4) Run/Room & Capacity Management

\n

Capacity-aware calendars prevent double-booking and help you assign runs or suites intelligently. Bonus points for visual run maps and litter-mate linking for households boarding multiple dogs.

\n

5) Automated Email/SMS Reminders

\n

Reduce no-shows and last-minute chaos with scheduled reminders for bookings, drop-offs, pickups, and balances due. It’s a win for customer satisfaction and staff sanity.

\n

6) Integrated Payments & Invoicing

\n

Integrated checkout simplifies deposits, balances, and refunds. Clear, itemized invoices (including upsells) prevent awkward conversations and speed up payment collection.

\n

7) Upsell & Add-On Workflows

\n

Increase average order value by offering baths, nail trims, enrichment sessions, daycare, or retail items during booking or check-in. The best systems make upsells one click.

\n

8) Staff Scheduling & Task Lists

\n

Even small teams benefit from shift planning and daily task boards (medications, feeding, walks). Look for role-based permissions and activity logs for accountability.

\n

9) Reporting & Analytics

\n

You need quick answers: occupancy by date, revenue by service, repeat customer %, and busiest lead sources. Export to CSV and filter by location if you have multiple sites.

\n

10) Mobile-Optimized Experience

\n

From the owner portal to the staff dashboard, mobile is essential. Managers should be able to approve bookings and check occupancy from a phone — especially on weekends.

How to Evaluate Vendors Against This Checklist

\n
    \n
  1. Map your workflows: Booking → vaccination → check-in → stay tasks → checkout. Note pain points.
    2. \n
  2. Shortlist 2–3 vendors: Ensure they cover the 10 must-haves; prioritize booking, vaccines, portal, and payments.
    3. \n
  3. Run real tasks in trial: Enter a booking with deposit, upload a vaccine, add an upsell, issue an invoice.
    4. \n
  4. Check support & onboarding: Response times, migration help, and staff training are critical.
    5. \n
  5. Consider growth: If you plan to add daycare or grooming, choose a platform that won’t need replacing.
