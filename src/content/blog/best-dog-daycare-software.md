## What to Look for in Dog Daycare Software

Before reviewing individual platforms, it helps to agree on what "best" actually means for a daycare operation.

### Online Booking and Capacity Management

A booking portal that shows real-time availability and enforces group-size caps prevents the two most common daycare problems: phone-tag with clients and accidental overbooking. Look for half-day and full-day pricing support, waitlist management, and the ability to block off capacity for specific playgroups.

### Automated Client Communication

Appointment reminders, vaccination expiry alerts, and daily report cards should send themselves. If your software requires staff to manually trigger these, the communication will be inconsistent — and inconsistency costs you clients. The best platforms send reminders at configurable intervals without anyone pressing a button.

### Vaccination and Health Record Management

Every dog that walks through your door needs up-to-date vaccinations. Software should store records, flag expiries before they happen, and optionally block check-in for dogs with lapsed vaccinations. Manual tracking in a spreadsheet is a compliance risk that grows with every new client.

### Payment Processing

Look for deposit collection at booking, package bundles (e.g., 10-day passes), automated recurring billing for regular clients, and card-on-file for easy end-of-day checkout. Payment should happen through the same system as booking — not bolted on via a separate app.

### Pricing Transparency

This one matters more than it appears in demos. Platforms that charge a flat monthly rate are predictable. Platforms that layer per-booking or per-transaction fees on top of a monthly subscription become expensive fast once your booking volume grows. Calculate your total cost at your current volume, not just the headline subscription price.

### 1. petcare.software — Best for Independent UK Operators

**Standout feature:** Same-day setup with a genuinely clean interface. Most independent daycares are fully operational within a few hours of signing up. Vaccination tracking with automatic expiry alerts is built into the base plan, not an add-on.

**Main limitation:** Fewer third-party integrations than established US-market platforms. If you rely on QuickBooks for accounting or specific POS hardware, verify compatibility before committing. petcare.software is purpose-built for UK pet care businesses, which means the pricing is in sterling, support is UK-based, and the feature set matches what an independent daycare actually needs rather than what a US franchise operation requires.

### 2. Gingr — Best for Multi-Location Operations

**Best for:** Larger facilities and multi-location operations with complex workflows.

**Pricing:** From £99/month plus per-booking fees (costs rise significantly at higher volume).

**Standout feature:** Enterprise-grade CRM with deep customisation, integrated retail POS, and comprehensive reporting across multiple locations. If you need to manage staff permissions, run detailed occupancy reports, and track retail sales alongside pet care services, Gingr delivers.

**Main limitation:** Complex onboarding. Expect to spend several days in setup and training before your team is comfortable. Customer support response times vary. The per-booking fee structure means your monthly cost is harder to predict as volume grows.

Gingr is well-established and has a large user community, which means plenty of third-party guides and a mature feature set. It is overkill for a single-location independent daycare but the right choice for an operator running two or more sites.

### 3. DoggieDashboard — Best for Budget-Conscious Single Facilities

**Best for:** Small daycares that want something dependable without paying for features they will never use.

**Pricing:** From £45/month with straightforward pricing tiers.

**Standout feature:** Clean booking calendar and daily schedule view. The interface is simple enough that new staff can learn it in under an hour, which reduces training overhead in high-turnover environments.

**Main limitation:** Limited automation compared to newer platforms. Reminder sequences and report cards require more manual input than petcare.software or Gingr. Advanced reporting and retail POS are not available.

DoggieDashboard has a loyal following among independent owners who tried more complex platforms and found they were paying for features they never used. If your operation is straightforward and you want something that just works, it is a reasonable choice.

### 4. PawPartner — Best for Client-Facing Transparency

**Best for:** Facilities where the client experience and parent communication are a competitive differentiator.

**Pricing:** From £55/month with transparent flat-rate pricing.

**Standout feature:** Live webcam integration and a polished owner portal. Pet parents can check in on their dogs during the day, view report cards in real time, and see vaccination status — all without calling the front desk. This drives loyalty and referral rates noticeably.

**Main limitation:** US-focused platform; pricing and support are oriented toward the North American market. Some management features are less developed than competitors. Works well for daycare and boarding; facilities with complex grooming or training operations may find it limiting.

### 5. TimeToPet — Best for Mixed-Service Businesses

**Best for:** Businesses that combine daycare with dog walking, pet sitting, or in-home services.

**Pricing:** From £30/month — one of the most affordable options in the category.

**Standout feature:** Scheduling and route management for mobile services. If your business model includes in-home visits or dog walking alongside facility-based daycare, TimeToPet handles both in a single platform better than any dedicated daycare platform.

**Main limitation:** Per-booking add-on fees apply at higher volumes, which makes total cost unpredictable for busy daycare facilities. The boarding and kennel features are functional but not as polished as platforms built specifically for facility-based care. Primarily US-focused.
