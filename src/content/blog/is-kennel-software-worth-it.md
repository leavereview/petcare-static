Investing in kennel software can feel like buying a brand-new dog bed: at first, you may wonder if it's necessary, but once you see how comfortable it is, you won't look back. Let's weigh the pros and cons to help you decide if the investment is worth it.

Our [kennel software](/kennel-software) page covers what these platforms do if you want the full picture first.

## Understanding the Investment

**Subscription & setup costs**

Most software charges a monthly or yearly fee. Some offer tiered plans, so you can start small and expand later. Look out for setup fees or training costs.

**Time for implementation**

Implementing new software takes time. There's data entry, staff training and perhaps a learning curve, but the payoff often outweighs the initial effort.

**Training and support**

Quality customer support matters. Choose a platform that offers onboarding sessions, tutorials and responsive help channels.

## Tangible Benefits

**Reduced administrative time**

Automation frees you from manual scheduling, invoicing and reminder tasks. More automation means more time for tail wagging and human-dog interaction.

**Improved accuracy & compliance**

Software keeps vaccination records, medication schedules and feeding notes organized, reducing mistakes and ensuring compliance with health requirements.

**Better customer service & communication**

Automatic reminders and customer portals keep pet parents informed and reassured. Happy customers are loyal customers.

## Intangible Benefits

**Professional image & brand trust**

A sleek booking experience and timely communications demonstrate professionalism, building trust in your brand.

**Data-driven decision making**

Reports and analytics show occupancy rates, revenue trends and seasonal patterns, helping you make informed decisions.

**Employee satisfaction**

Simplified processes make work less stressful. When staff know what they're doing and why, morale goes up.

## ROI Calculation for Small Businesses

1. **Estimating time savings**: Add up hours spent on administrative tasks now and compare them to the streamlined process with software.
2. **Revenue impacts & upsells**: Better organization can lead to more bookings and the ability to offer add-on services like grooming or training.
3. **Long-term scalability**: Investing early sets you up for future growth. When you're ready to expand, your software can grow with you.

## Conclusion: Is It Worth It?

For many small businesses, the time savings, reduced errors and improved customer experience more than justify the subscription cost. While spreadsheets and basic tools may work initially, they often lack essential features like vaccination tracking and reporting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is kennel software and who needs it?

Kennel software is a specialized management system designed for pet boarding facilities, kennels, and multi-service pet businesses. It automates booking, scheduling, client communication, and record-keeping. Small kennels with even 5-10 runs benefit significantly because the software eliminates manual spreadsheets, reduces booking errors, and provides professional client communication.

### How much does kennel software typically cost?

For small pet businesses, kennel software typically costs between £29-£69 per month ($35-$85 USD) for entry-level plans that cover essential features like booking, scheduling, and client management. Mid-tier plans ($100-$150/month) add features like automated marketing, advanced reporting, and multi-user access.

### What are the essential features of kennel management software?

Essential features include online booking (24/7 customer access), automated email and SMS reminders to reduce no-shows, basic pet profile management with vaccination tracking, simple run/kennel assignment, payment processing with deposits, and a client portal where owners can view bookings and update pet information.

### How long does it take to set up kennel software?

For small kennels, expect 1-2 weeks from purchase to full operation. Initial setup takes 3-5 hours and can be done in one sitting. Staff training for small teams (1-5 people) requires 1-2 hours per person, usually accomplished in one training session.

## The Real Cost of Running a Kennel Without Software

The cost of not using software is rarely framed this way, but it's worth doing the maths.

Consider a 20-dog kennel running a fully manual operation. A typical week looks like this:

| Task | Time per day | Weekly total |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Scheduling and booking calls | 45 min | 3.75 hrs |
| Client reminder calls | 30 min | 2.5 hrs |
| Vaccination checks before arrivals | 20 min | 1.4 hrs |
| Invoicing and payment collection | — | 1 hr/week |
| No-show and late cancellation follow-up | — | 30 min/week |
| **Total** | | **~9 hours/week** |

At minimum wage (£11.44/hour in 2024/25), that's approximately £104 per week in staff or owner time — over £440 per month.

A kennel management platform typically costs £50–100 per month for a facility of this size. The net monthly saving is £340–390, not counting the additional revenue from better occupancy (no double-bookings, online booking capturing demand you'd have otherwise missed) and reduced no-shows from automated deposits and reminders.

The payback period for kennel software at this scale is typically measured in weeks, not months.

## What Kennel Software Actually Does Day-to-Day

It helps to walk through what a typical day looks like with software handling the operational layer.

**Morning:** Staff open the dashboard and see a list of today's arrivals and departures, each with run assignment, vaccination status, and any care notes. No paper checking. No verbal briefing to reconstruct who's booked in. Today's feeding and medication tasks are pre-populated and ready to tick off.

**During the day:** Any dog with an upcoming vaccination expiry has been automatically flagged and the owner notified days ago. New bookings arrive via the online booking widget without a phone call. A deposit has already been collected and the confirmation sent automatically.

**Evening:** Staff mark feeding and medication tasks as completed from their phone. Care notes for any dogs flagged during the day are visible to the incoming shift. Tomorrow's arrivals and departures are visible at a glance. Handover takes five minutes instead of twenty.

**End of week:** Invoicing is automated — amounts calculated from the booking dates and services, payments collected via the platform. The week's vaccination compliance report is available if a council inspection were called.

This is what running a kennel with software looks like. The operational friction doesn't disappear entirely, but it shifts from constant manual effort to exception handling.

## When Kennel Software Is NOT Worth It

Honesty matters here.

If you are boarding fewer than five dogs per week on a casual, informal basis — mainly for friends and family — the ROI is marginal. The overhead of learning and maintaining a management platform may not justify the time saved on what is already a low-volume operation.

Similarly, if you operate as a fully solo, appointment-only facility with long-term clients you know personally and have no plans to grow, a simple spreadsheet and reminder calendar may genuinely be sufficient.

Kennel software earns its cost at scale: when you're handling 15+ bookings per week, managing multiple staff members, running online availability, and needing vaccination records that will survive a council inspection.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does kennel software cost per month?

Entry-level plans for small kennels typically start at £30–60 per month. Mid-tier plans covering all core features (online booking, vaccination tracking, staff task lists, reporting) range from £60–120 per month. Purpose-built UK kennel platforms with AAL compliance features and no per-transaction fees sit in the £70–100 range for most independent facilities.

### Is kennel software difficult to set up?

Most platforms designed for independent kennels are configured in one to two days. You'll need to enter your kennel runs or rooms, set up your vaccination policy, import existing client records, and connect your payment processing. Vendors typically provide onboarding support. Staff training for daily operations usually takes an hour or two.

### Can my clients book online through kennel software?

Yes. A booking widget embeds on your website or links from your Google Business Profile. Clients select dates, choose a run type, enter their pet's details, and pay a deposit without needing to call you. Availability is updated in real time, preventing double-bookings.

### Does kennel software handle vaccination tracking?

Yes. Vaccination certificates are stored against each pet profile with expiry dates tracked automatically. The system sends owners reminder emails before vaccines expire and can block new bookings from pets with lapsed certificates. This replaces manual certificate checking before every check-in.

### What happens to my data if I cancel?

Reputable platforms allow you to export your client and pet data in standard formats (CSV or similar) before cancelling. Confirm the data export policy before signing up — this is a critical question when evaluating any SaaS platform where your customer relationships are stored.
