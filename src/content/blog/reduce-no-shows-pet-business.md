## Understanding Why Clients No-Show

Before solving the problem, understand it:

- **Forgot the booking** - Life gets busy
- **Plans changed** - But they didn't think to tell you
- **Found alternative care** - A friend or family member became available
- **Pet got sick** - Legitimate reason, but still hurts your business
- **No consequence** - If there's no penalty, why bother canceling?

## Strategy 1: Require Deposits

Deposits are the single most effective way to reduce no-shows.

### How to Implement
- Charge 25-50% of the booking value at time of booking
- Make deposits non-refundable within 24-48 hours of the appointment
- Allow deposits to be applied to rescheduled bookings

### What to Expect
- **Initial resistance**: Some clients will push back
- **Better clients**: Those who do book are more committed
- **Fewer no-shows**: Industry average drops from 10-15% to 2-5%

### How to Position It
"Deposits help us hold your spot and ensure we have appropriate staff coverage. Your deposit is fully applied to your booking."

## Strategy 2: Automated Reminders

People genuinely forget. Help them remember with:

### Reminder Timeline
- **At booking**: Confirmation email with all details
- **1 week before**: "Your booking is coming up" email
- **2 days before**: SMS reminder with booking details
- **Day before**: Final confirmation request

### What to Include
- Date, time, and duration
- What to bring (vaccination records, food, medications)
- Cancellation policy reminder
- Easy way to confirm or cancel

## Strategy 3: Easy Cancellation Process

Make it easier to cancel than to no-show:

- **Online cancellation** through customer portal
- **Text-to-cancel** option
- **Clear deadlines** for penalty-free cancellation
- **Reschedule option** to retain the booking

If canceling is as simple as clicking a button, clients will cancel rather than ghost you.

## Strategy 4: Waitlist Management

Turn cancellations into revenue:

- Maintain a waitlist for popular days
- Contact waitlist clients immediately when spots open
- Offer small incentives for waitlist clients who can book last-minute

Good [kennel software](/kennel-software/) automates this entire process.

## Strategy 5: Enforce Your Policies

Policies only work if enforced:

### First No-Show
- Charge the deposit
- Send a friendly reminder of your policy
- Require deposits for future bookings

### Repeat Offenders
- Require full payment upfront
- Consider declining future bookings
- Document everything

### Be Consistent
Apply the same rules to everyone. Exceptions undermine your policy and create resentment from clients who do follow the rules.

## Strategy 6: Make Booking Valuable

Create incentives to keep bookings:

- **Loyalty programs** - Reward consistent bookers
- **Package pricing** - Prepaid packages reduce no-shows significantly
- **Add-on services** - Clients who've added grooming or training are less likely to cancel
