Boarding kennel software does something general pet management tools can't: it manages physical space. A dog daycare platform tracks how many dogs are booked for a session. A boarding kennel platform tracks which specific run each dog is sleeping in tonight, whether it's been cleaned, what was in its food bowl, and whether its vaccinations are still current.

That distinction matters more than it might initially seem. When you're running a facility with 20 or 30 runs and dogs checking in and out across multiple nights at different times, you're not managing appointments — you're managing a live inventory of occupied and unoccupied physical spaces, each with its own occupant's care requirements.

The right [boarding kennel software](/kennel-software) handles this complexity without requiring a whiteboard, a spreadsheet, and three Post-it notes per dog. This guide covers what separates good boarding kennel software from general pet management tools, the features that actually matter, and how to evaluate your options before committing to a platform.

## Core Features Every Boarding Kennel Needs

Not all pet management software is built for overnight boarding. These are the capabilities that matter specifically for kennel operations.

### Capacity and Room Management

The foundation of any boarding kennel platform is a visual map of your runs or rooms. You need to see, at a glance, which runs are occupied tonight, which are being cleaned, which are available for tomorrow's arrivals, and whether you have runs of the right size for incoming bookings.

This visual capacity management prevents double-bookings more reliably than a shared calendar does. When every run is a named unit in the system with its own booking history, the platform can tell you whether Run 7 — your large-dog outdoor run — is available Thursday through Sunday, not just whether you have "a large run" free somewhere. For busy periods like Christmas and bank holidays when demand outstrips capacity, this level of detail is the difference between managing demand confidently and turning away business you don't need to turn away.

Blocking runs for deep cleaning, maintenance, or quarantine is a single action that prevents bookings from being accepted for that period without any manual calendar management.

### Multi-Night Pricing and Discount Rules

Boarding kennels have pricing structures that daycare software doesn't account for: nightly rates rather than session rates, discounts for extended stays, multi-dog household pricing, and premium run uplifts. A boarding kennel platform should handle all of these automatically.

When a client books a 10-night stay for two dogs in a premium run, the system should calculate the correct total — applying multi-dog and extended-stay discounts where they apply, adding any extras like grooming or additional enrichment activities — and collect a deposit at booking without staff involvement. Platforms that can't handle these structures push the complexity back onto staff, creating manual calculation errors and inconsistent pricing.

### Feeding and Medication Schedules Per Dog

Every dog in your facility has its own feeding requirements. Some have twice-daily meals with specific quantities; others have three smaller meals, allergies, or supplements. Dogs on medication need doses logged at specific times, with the completion timestamped and attributed to the staff member who administered it.

A boarding kennel platform maintains these schedules on each dog's profile and generates a daily task list for staff — what to feed, when, in what quantity, and what medication to administer and when. Staff mark tasks as completed from their phone. This log is visible to owners via the portal and provides an audit trail for any health queries during or after the stay. For facilities operating under UK Animal Activity Licensing, timestamped care logs are part of the record set that inspectors review.

### Vaccination Requirement Enforcement

Taking a booking from a dog with an expired vaccination is a liability risk and a welfare risk to every other dog in your facility. Good boarding kennel software enforces vaccination compliance at the booking stage — not at check-in, when it's too late to do anything about it.

When a client tries to book, the system checks whether their dog's required vaccinations are current. If they're not, the booking is blocked until a valid certificate is uploaded. Automatic reminders go out 30 and 60 days before expiry, reducing the number of clients who arrive at check-in with lapsed records. This protection runs automatically without requiring staff to remember to check every booking against a paper certificate folder.

### Automated Reminders and Report Cards to Owners

Automated communication reduces the volume of calls your staff handle during stays. Booking confirmations, pre-arrival reminders with vaccination requirements, day-before check-in reminders, and post-checkout messages all run automatically without staff action.

For clients with anxious dogs — and there are many — daily photo updates and brief care notes sent through the owner portal eliminate most "how is my dog?" calls. Report cards take staff less than two minutes to send from a phone. Their impact on client retention and word-of-mouth referrals is disproportionate to the effort involved.

## Boarding Kennel Software vs Day Care Software — What's Different

The platforms overlap significantly, but the differences matter if overnight boarding is your core business.

| Feature | Boarding kennel | Day care only |
|---------|----------------|---------------|
| Overnight capacity management | ✓ | ✗ |
| Multi-night pricing | ✓ | ✗ |
| Daily report cards | ✓ | ✓ |
| Check-in/check-out | ✓ | ✓ |
| Feeding schedules per dog | ✓ | Partial |
| Online booking | ✓ | ✓ |
| Vaccination tracking | ✓ | ✓ |

If you offer both overnight boarding and daycare from the same facility, you need a platform built to handle both service types from a single client profile. Clients who bring their dog for daycare and occasional boarding shouldn't need to manage two separate accounts or upload their pet's information twice. A combined platform also gives you consolidated reporting across service types, which matters for business planning and pricing reviews.

Day care platforms that add a "boarding module" after the fact often have weaker run management and multi-night pricing logic than platforms built for boarding from the ground up. If overnight boarding is your primary service, evaluate platforms on their boarding capabilities first and check the daycare functionality as a secondary consideration.

## How to Evaluate Boarding Kennel Software

**Number of runs or rooms.** Most platforms tier their pricing by facility size. Confirm where you sit in their pricing structure now and what the cost looks like if you expand by 30–50%.

**Combined boarding and daycare.** If you offer both, look for a platform that manages both services under unified client profiles. Running separate systems creates data duplication, a worse client experience, and more administrative overhead than you'd expect.

**Accounting integration.** QuickBooks and Xero are the two most common integrations. If you manage your books in either, check whether the platform exports invoices and payment records automatically or requires a manual export. For larger facilities handling hundreds of bookings per month, the difference between automatic sync and manual export is several hours a week.

**Client portal quality.** The owner portal is what your clients interact with directly. Test it thoroughly before committing — can clients easily upload vaccination certificates, view upcoming bookings, see care updates during a stay, and manage their pet's profile? A poor portal increases inbound calls and email volume.

**Mobile access for staff.** Evening checks, weekend handovers, and outdoor run cleaning all happen away from a desk. Staff need to be able to log care tasks, view the day's schedule, and send report cards from a phone. Confirm the mobile experience is genuinely usable and not just a scaled-down version of a desktop interface that staff will avoid using.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is boarding kennel software?

Boarding kennel software is a management platform built for pet facilities that offer overnight stays. It handles online booking, run or room assignment, multi-night pricing, feeding and medication schedules per dog, vaccination compliance enforcement, and automated owner communication. Unlike general appointment booking tools, it manages physical space — tracking which specific runs are occupied and by which dogs across multiple nights.

### How much does boarding kennel software cost?

Pricing typically ranges from £30–60 per month for small kennels to £80–150 per month for medium facilities offering both boarding and daycare. Most platforms tier pricing by number of runs, staff users, or monthly bookings. Per-transaction fees add up at peak periods — flat-rate plans are worth looking for if your volume spikes over Christmas and summer.

### Can boarding kennel software handle both overnight and daycare?

Yes. Most modern boarding kennel platforms manage both service types from a single dashboard with unified client profiles. Look for combined reporting across boarding and daycare revenue and a booking flow that distinguishes between overnight and day-session availability without requiring separate systems.

### Does boarding kennel software integrate with QuickBooks?

Many platforms offer QuickBooks or Xero integrations. Quality varies — some sync in real time, others require a manual export. Test the integration specifically during any free trial rather than relying on the vendor's feature list. For high-volume facilities, an unreliable accounting sync creates more work than it saves.

### Is boarding kennel software difficult to set up?

Most platforms designed for independent kennels can be fully configured in one to two days. Setup involves entering your kennel runs, configuring vaccination policy and booking rules, importing client records, and connecting payment processing. The boarding-specific elements — multi-night pricing rules, run assignment logic, feeding schedule templates — take more time than a simple booking tool but are well within reach for a non-technical operator.
