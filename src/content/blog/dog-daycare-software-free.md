## What Free Dog Daycare Software Actually Exists

### Google Sheets and Spreadsheet Templates

The most widely used "free" solution is a well-structured Google Sheets template. You can track bookings, client details, vaccination dates, and payments in a shared spreadsheet accessible from any device. Templates exist online — search for "dog daycare booking spreadsheet" to find community-built options.

**What spreadsheets can do:**
- Track client and pet information
- Log booking dates and payments manually
- Monitor vaccination expiry dates with conditional formatting
- Share access with staff in real time

**What spreadsheets cannot do:**
- Send automated booking confirmations to clients
- Block overbooking based on real-time capacity
- Generate report cards or daily updates
- Process payments or invoices
- Send vaccination reminder emails automatically
- Provide an online booking portal for clients

### Free Booking Tools (Calendly, Setmore)

General-purpose booking tools like Calendly or Setmore have free tiers. They let clients book appointments online without phone calls. However, they are not built for pet care and lack:

- Dog/pet profiles
- Vaccination tracking
- Capacity limits by playgroup or kennel bay
- Report cards
- Multi-pet per booking support

They work as a stopgap but quickly become frustrating as your business grows.

## The Real Cost of Free Software

Free tools have a hidden cost: your time. Consider what "free" actually costs a daycare running 15–20 dogs per day:

**Admin time per week without software:**
- Manually confirming bookings by phone or email: 3–5 hours
- Chasing vaccination documents before each visit: 1–2 hours
- Sending invoices and chasing payment: 1–2 hours
- Preparing and sending daily report cards: 2–3 hours
- Answering "what time do you close?" phone calls: 1 hour

That's 8–13 hours of admin per week — time you're not spending with dogs, growing the business, or resting.

**No-show rates without automated reminders:**

Facilities without automated booking reminders typically see no-show rates of 15–25%. At £25/day per dog, three no-shows per week costs £75 in lost revenue — roughly £3,600 per year. A software subscription at £79/month costs £948 per year. The maths makes itself.

**At scale, spreadsheets break:**

When you're managing 5 dogs, a spreadsheet is manageable. At 15–20 dogs with recurring clients, multiple staff members, and complex scheduling (playgroups, daycare vs boarding, add-ons), spreadsheets create mistakes. Overbooking happens. Vaccination records get missed. Invoices go unsent. These aren't hypothetical — they're the reasons most growing daycares eventually switch to paid software.

## When Free Software Makes Sense

Free tools are a legitimate starting point in specific circumstances:

**Under 5 dogs per day:** At this scale, a shared Google Sheet and a WhatsApp group for reminders genuinely works. The admin overhead is manageable and paid software would be hard to justify financially.

**Testing your business model:** If you're running a trial period before committing fully to daycare, free tools let you validate demand without ongoing costs.

**Side operation alongside another business:** If daycare is a small add-on to grooming or training, free tools may be sufficient until volume warrants investment.

## When It Stops Making Sense

The tipping point is usually one of the following:

**10+ dogs per day:** At this volume, manual booking management becomes a part-time job in itself. Capacity errors and no-shows become expensive rather than inconvenient.

**5+ admin hours per week:** If you or a staff member is spending more than five hours per week on booking admin, the software pays for itself in labour costs before accounting for anything else.

**Vaccination compliance risk:** Councils increasingly audit vaccination records during inspections. Spreadsheets can have gaps; purpose-built software maintains complete, timestamped records.

**Repeat clients with complex needs:** Regular clients booking weeks ahead, managing cancellations, deposits, and add-ons in a spreadsheet is error-prone. One double-booking or missed deposit refund can damage client trust.
